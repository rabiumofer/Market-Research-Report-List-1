<p><p>The chickpea market is primarily divided into two types: Kabuli and Desi chickpeas. Kabuli chickpeas are larger, lighter-colored, and have a smooth coat, commonly used in salads and hummus. Their market often sees higher prices due to their popularity in Western cuisine. Desi chickpeas, smaller, darker, and with a rough texture, are mainly used in Indian and Middle Eastern dishes. They are more resilient and generally have a lower market price, appealing to diverse culinary applications.</p></p>
